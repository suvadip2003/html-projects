Swami Vivakananda

Swami Vivekananda, a renowned spiritual leader and disciple of Ramakrishna, introduced Indian philosophies like Vedanta and Yoga to the West. He emphasized self-realization, universal brotherhood, and inspired youth through his speeches, especially his famous address at the 1893 Chicago Parliament.

About the Legend

  • Born on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India.
  • Disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, a key influence in his spiritual journey.
  • Introduced Hindu philosophy and Yoga to the Western world.
  • Delivered a famous speech at the 1893 Parliament of Religions in Chicago.
  • Emphasized the importance of self-realization and personal development.
  • Founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 to promote social service and education.
  • Advocated for the unity of all religions and universal brotherhood.
  • Wrote extensively on spirituality, philosophy, and social issues.
  • Influenced many leaders and thinkers, inspiring a generation of reformers.
  • Passed away on January 19, 1902, leaving a lasting legacy.
